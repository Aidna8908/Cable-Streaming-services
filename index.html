The Core Problem

Why are we all overspending?

Streaming was supposed to be the affordable alternative to cable. But as services multiply, merge, and raise their prices, the dream of "cutting the cord" has quietly become just as expensive — or worse. Here's the big picture.

01

Subscription Creep

Each service costs around $15–$25/month. Subscribing to just 5–6 platforms easily exceeds a traditional cable bill. Most households don't track how many they're actually paying for.

02

Corporate Consolidation

Disney owns Hulu, ABC, ESPN, and Fox content. Amazon, Netflix, and a handful of others control the majority of the market — leaving little room for price competition.

03

Rising Content Costs

Netflix is spending $400 million on a single season of Stranger Things. Those costs get passed directly to subscribers through annual price hikes.

04

The Sports Problem

Live sports — the last big advantage cable had — is now scattered across Netflix, Amazon, Peacock, ESPN+, and more. Getting full coverage means paying for all of them.

"Live-TV streaming platforms like Hulu + Live TV and YouTube TV hover around $75–$95 per month — nearly on par with traditional cable, but without the cost savings many expected."
— Boardroom.tv, "The Economics of Streaming vs. Cable in 2025"
